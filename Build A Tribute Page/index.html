Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Here's a timeline of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1914 - Born in Cresco, Iowa
  • 1933 - Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Minnesota, thanks to a Depression era program known as the "National Youth Administration"
  • 1939 - Receives a Ph.D. in Genetics from the University of Colorado
  • 1940 - Writes a book with his colleagues at the University of Minnesota and marries his wife of 69 years, Margret Gibson. They have three children
  • 1941 - Divorces his wife's homemaker and marries his second wife
  • 1942 - Realizes that he must have electoral votes and becomes a professor at Texas A&M University
  • 1943 - Leaves his family to become his true love's second marriage
  • 1944 - Realizes that he must have electoral votes and becomes a professor at the University of Texas at Austin
  • 1945 - Receives his Ph.D. in Medicine and dies at the age of 95
"It is during our darkest moments that we must focus to see the light."
